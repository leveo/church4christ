---
title: Serving as Worship
author: Pastor David Chen
date: "2026-03-08"
excerpt: The person stacking chairs and the person leading the songs are doing the same holy thing. Here's why.
---

On a recent Sunday I watched two things happen at once. At the front, our worship team led the congregation in singing, hands lifted, voices full. At the back, a quiet man I'll call Brother Wang was on his knees, wiping up a child's spilled juice before anyone slipped on it. I don't think Brother Wang would have called what he was doing worship. But I would.

We tend to draw a line between the "spiritual" parts of church — singing, praying, preaching — and the "practical" parts — parking, cleaning, counting, cooking. On one side, worship; on the other, mere logistics. The Bible refuses to draw that line.

> "Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men." — Colossians 3:23

Notice the word "whatever." Paul is writing to ordinary people about ordinary work, and he says it can all be done "for the Lord." The greeter at the door, the volunteer in the nursery, the person setting up chairs at seven in the morning — they are not the support staff for worship. They are worshiping.

This matters, because most of us will never lead from the stage, and we can start to feel like second-class members, watching the "real" ministry from the sidelines. But there is no sideline in God's economy. The kingdom is built quietly, by unseen hands, doing small things with great love.

So if you're serving somewhere no one sees, take heart. God sees. And what you're doing is not less than worship — it is worship, offered with your hands.
