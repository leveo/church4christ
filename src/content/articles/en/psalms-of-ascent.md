---
title: Walking Through the Psalms of Ascent
author: Pastor Grace Lin
date: "2026-02-15"
excerpt: Fifteen short psalms, sung on the road to worship — and what they teach us about the long walk of faith.
---

Tucked into the middle of the Bible is a small collection of fifteen psalms, each one labeled "A Song of Ascents." Ancient worshipers sang them as they climbed the road up to Jerusalem for the yearly feasts. Imagine a dusty crowd of pilgrims, families and strangers together, singing their way uphill. These were the road-songs of God's people.

What strikes me is how honest they are. They don't begin at the mountaintop. The first one opens in distress: "In my trouble I cried to the Lord." The journey to worship starts, as ours often does, from a hard place.

But as the songs go on, something shifts. There is a psalm about waiting through a sleepless night. One about the tears of sowing giving way to the joy of harvest. One, the shortest of all, about simply stilling a restless heart, "like a weaned child with its mother." Step by step, the pilgrims climb — not by denying their troubles, but by carrying them Godward.

> "I lift up my eyes to the hills. From where does my help come? My help comes from the Lord, who made heaven and earth." — Psalm 121:1-2

That's the pattern of faith, isn't it? Not a single heroic leap, but a long walk, uphill, in company, singing as we go. Some days our voices are strong; other days we can barely hum along, and we lean on the family walking beside us.

Over the coming weeks I want us to walk these fifteen psalms together, one at a time. Bring your troubles with you. That's exactly where the road begins.
