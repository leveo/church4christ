---
title: The Discipline of Prayer
author: Pastor Grace Lin
date: "2026-04-19"
excerpt: Most of us feel guilty about prayer. What if the way forward is not more guilt, but a gentler, steadier habit?
---

Whenever I mention prayer, I watch a familiar look cross people's faces — a small wince of guilt. Almost everyone I meet feels they should pray more, and almost no one feels they pray enough. If that's you, please hear this first: God is not standing over you with a stopwatch, disappointed. He is a Father, delighted that you've come at all.

But I do want to talk about prayer as a discipline, because I think we've quietly given up on it. We wait to feel inspired, and inspiration rarely comes, so we pray only in emergencies. A discipline is different. It's what we keep doing when the feeling isn't there — not to earn God's love, but to stay in the place where we can receive it.

> "Pray without ceasing." — 1 Thessalonians 5:17

That verse can sound impossible until you realize Paul isn't asking for round-the-clock intensity. He's describing a life turned Godward, a running conversation woven through the day. A sentence of thanks over lunch. A quiet plea in the elevator. A name lifted up while stuck in traffic.

Start smaller than you think you should. Pick one fixed moment in your day — the first sip of coffee, the walk to the bus — and simply show up there with God, whether you feel like it or not. Some days will feel dry and dutiful. Keep coming anyway. Rivers carve canyons not by force but by returning to the same place, day after day.

The goal is not a perfect prayer life. The goal is a Father you keep talking to. Begin again today.
